# Sample Story Memo

Working title: The Clockmaker Under Glass.

Mira Vale repairs antique clocks in a floodlit arcade that closes every night at 9:17. Her missing brother left a brass moth in the workshop drawer and a note that says the city council has been selling minutes from abandoned lives. The old observatory above North Bell Station still rings at noon even though its bell was removed during the winter curfew.

People: Mira Vale, Toma Vale, Councillor Iven Rusk, the archivist known as Sister Hal.
Places: North Bell Station, the glass arcade, the old observatory, the canal market.
Objects: brass moth, ledger of minutes, cracked station clock, black ribbon key.

The story should feel intimate and strange, but not explain the magic too early. Need to decide whether Toma is alive, whether the council is villainous or desperate, and whether the brass moth is a spy, a key, or a memory container.

Possible ending: Mira refuses to restore the city's stolen time and instead gives one lost hour back to every person who was erased from the records.
