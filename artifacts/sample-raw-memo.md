# Sample Raw Memo: The Clockmaker Under Glass

Working title: The Clockmaker Under Glass.

Mira Vale repairs clocks in a glass arcade that closes every night at 9:17. Her missing brother Toma left a brass moth in the workshop drawer with a note claiming the city council has been selling minutes from abandoned lives.

The old observatory above North Bell Station still rings at noon even though its bell was removed during the winter curfew. Mira thinks the sound is a memory leaking through the gears, but the memo must not decide whether that theory is true.

The council keeps a ledger of minutes in a locked cabinet under the arcade. The ledger may be proof, bait, or a false record. Toma's fate is unresolved. The brass moth truth is unresolved. The Council motive is unresolved.

Possible ending: Mira may return one lost hour to every erased person, but the ending depends on Toma fate and Council motive. Do not explain the magic too early, do not turn the brass moth into a final answer, and do not make the council simply evil unless a human author chooses that route.
